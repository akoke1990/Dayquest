# DayQuest — Privacy Policy (DRAFT)

**Status:** Draft for review — NOT legal advice. Have a professional review before
public/App Store launch. Good enough to brief testers now.
**Last updated:** 2026-06-21

DayQuest helps you explore real places near you. We try to collect as little as
possible. Here's the plain-English version.

## What we collect
- **Location:** your device's GPS, used only while you're using the app, to find
  places near you and confirm you've arrived at a stop. We do not track you in the
  background or build a history of everywhere you go.
- **Photos:** photos you take for a quest are stored on your device. If you choose to
  share a recap, you control where it goes.
- **Anonymous usage data:** an anonymous app install ID and basic events (e.g. quest
  started/completed, points earned) so we can understand whether the app works and is
  fun. This is not tied to your identity.
- **Account (optional):** if you sign in with Google or Apple, we store your name,
  email, and avatar, plus your game progress (points, quests, streak), so it syncs
  across devices. Signing in is optional — the app works fully without an account.

## What we do NOT do
- We do **not** sell your data.
- We do **not** track your location in the background.
- We do **not** post anything on your behalf — sharing is always your explicit choice.

## Third parties we use
- **Anthropic** (generates quest content), **Google Maps Platform** & **OpenStreetMap**
  (place data + maps), **Supabase** (optional account sign-in & storage). Each handles
  data under its own policy.

## Safety (please read)
DayQuest sends you to real, public places. **Stay aware of your surroundings, obey
traffic laws, don't trespass, and skip any stop that feels unsafe.** You explore at
your own risk and good judgment.

## Your choices
- Use the app anonymously (no account).
- Delete photos on your device anytime.
- Signed in? Request deletion of your account and profile data by contacting us.

## Age
DayQuest is intended for users **13 and older.**
